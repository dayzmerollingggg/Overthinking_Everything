Line Holder


Typical events at a college all end up in the same way: there is a long line that doesn’t seem to move at all and in the end most people get turned away. This is not ideal for the ones who actually do want to enter the event or get free food, but it’s perfect for you. 

How to: 

As a line holder, your job will be to stand in these long lines and “hold a spot” for others. Have a sign up (either strung around your neck or as a poster as shown in fig. 1) that reads “cut into my spot for $xxx”. Put whatever price best suits the average price a college student of your college would be willing to spend on a small luxury item. Imagine the amount of money an individual person would spend for an expensive dinner meal. At the bottom of the sign you could have payment options. Recommendations include cash, Venmo, PayPal, and Zelle (example given in fig. 2). 

Every time someone pays for your spot in line, quickly rush to the back of the line and start the process over again (fig. 3). This is the more ethical way. If you are not ethical, let the person in front of you but don’t move. This way is not recommended since you might be thrown out before making a lot of money (fig. 3). 

This method also allows for team effort. Gather some friends and crash the line (fig. 4)

Quick Notes:

  • Average hourly wage: $however much you list x 2
  • Supplies needed: marker, poster sign (make sure its sturdy), string (optional), phone (for online payments), wallet (for cash payments), any payment systems you want to set up
